the ribbon microphone

sep – nov ‘24
last september (as of jan ‘25), in a fit of pique, i sliced my modmic off its cable, then designed and built a ribbon microphone as a replacement. aside from some background hum, unavoidable since the ribbon-to-return-wire loop has nonzero area and i can't magnetically shield it myself, this device sounds (subjectively) like a studio mic. this was not rigorously tested in the least! i'm no expert on frequency response verification of microphones (and don't have an anechoic chamber to do it in, anyway). if you have reliable (and cheap?) techniques for mic analysis, let me know!
