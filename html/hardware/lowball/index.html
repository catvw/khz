the lowball

dec ‘24 – jan ‘25
ever wanted to control your cursor with your feet? no? well, now you can!

designing keyboards with integrated cursors, while something that i think should be done by default, is annoying — especially when the keyboard in question is a descendent of the extraordinary cluster keyboard of the ‘90s. so i figured “hey, why don't i just sidestep the entire problem and utilize another limb?” thus the lowball was born. the name was the result of a bit of back and forth with a friend, because the obvious name (“football”) is funny but ungoogleable and “pedoball just doesn't have the same ring to it.”

contents

the ball

my main keyboard is (as of this writing in jan ‘25) a charybdis nano, from the excellent quentin lebastard of bastardkb. if you're not yet ready for a cluster keyboard but want to take a wild leap in the right direction, i highly recommend it. or another dactyl-style board, but this one worked well for me long before i had a 3d printer. which has an integrated thumb trackball. i had changed the trackball from the original iridescent red plastic ball to a vinegar-etched steel ball bearing of the same diameter (a mod i heartily recommend!), and so resolved to try a steel ball again for this project.

however, a 76.2 mm diameter steel ball is a bit heavier than a 38 mm one (read: nearly 2 kg), so i opted to go with a lighter, but still unusual, option: wood.

the key cluster

with my right foot attended to, my attention turned to my left. perhaps i could offload the entirety of mouse functionality to my feet with a couple of keys?

so i did. this was mostly a process of mapping out how each part of my foot could move (inspired by the muscle & tendon architecture therein), then making prototypes which attempted to reflect that. i also wanted to be able to step into and out of the cluster without much difficulty.
